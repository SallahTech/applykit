Which landing page flow feels right?

All drive free signups, but the journey to get there is different.

A

Product-Led (Show, Don't Tell)

Hero with headline + live product demo (animated Kanban board + CV tailoring in action) → Social proof counter → 3 feature spotlights with inline mockups → Before/After CV comparison → Pricing → CTA

Vibe: "Look how good this is" — lets the product sell itself. Best when the product visuals are compelling (they are).
B

Pain → Solution (Story Arc)

Hero with pain-point headline ("Your resume gets filtered out 78% of the time") → Problem section (3 pain points) → "There's a better way" transition → Product showcase → Testimonials → How it works (3 steps) → Pricing → CTA

Vibe: Emotional hook first, product second. Classic SaaS conversion playbook — high empathy, high urgency.
C

Hybrid (Best of Both)

Hero with bold headline + animated product preview → Social proof bar → "How it works" 3-step flow → Live before/after CV demo → Feature grid (6 features) → Testimonials → Pricing cards → Final CTA with stats

Vibe: Leads with the product but weaves in the story. More sections = more scroll, but each section earns its place. My recommendation.
